Most owners we meet are paying more than they need to — not because of aggressive moves, but because of deductions they simply didn't know about. Here are five worth a conversation.

## 1. The home office deduction

If you use part of your home *regularly and exclusively* for business, you can deduct a portion of rent, utilities, and insurance. The simplified method is easy; the actual-expense method often saves more.

## 2. Vehicle and mileage

Track business miles and you can deduct them at the standard rate — or deduct actual costs. The key is contemporaneous records.

## 3. Retirement contributions

A SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k) can shelter a significant share of profit while building your own future.

## 4. Section 179 and bonus depreciation

Equipment, computers, and certain improvements can often be expensed in the year you buy them.

## 5. Health insurance premiums

Self-employed owners can frequently deduct premiums above the line.
